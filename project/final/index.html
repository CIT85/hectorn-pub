What is Light Pollution?

Light pollution occurs when excessive artificial light is present in the environment, often from streetlights, buildings, or other sources. This extra light can cause problems like blocking our view of stars and disrupting the natural rhythms of plants and animals. It's kind of like when you're trying to look at something in the dark, but someone shines a bright light in your eyes, making it hard to see.

Light pollution consists of

  • Glare
  • Skyglow
  • Light Tresspass
  • Clutter
  • Disruption of Ecosystems

How Bad is it?

Living in modern society you may have already experienced the effects of light pollution, such as having difficulty stargazing or disrupted sleep due to bright lights outside your window. Sharing these experiences, this issue is as common to you as your neighbor. You can see a more in depth break down on this page

Possible solutions

There are many possible solutions to the issue of Light Pollution. Here are some ways to mitigate the effects of light pollution, both at the individual level and within society as a whole:

Individual Actions

What YOU can do:
  1. Use Light Responsibly
  2. Choose Dark-Sky Friendly Lighting
  3. Use Warm-Colored Bulbs
  4. Close Curtains and Blinds
  5. Support Dark Sky-Friendly Policies

Societal Actions

What WE can do:
  1. Implement Outdoor Lighting Regulations
  2. Educate the Public
  3. Promote Energy Efficiency
  4. Preserve Dark Sky Areas
  5. Collaborate Across Sectors
