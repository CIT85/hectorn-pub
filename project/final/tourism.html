Light pollution impacts tourism

Overall, light pollution can detract from the unique experiences that tourists seek, whether it's the opportunity to observe the night sky, connect with nature, explore cultural heritage, or promote health and well-being

How?

Astronomy tourism: Excessive artificial light can diminish the visibility of stars and celestial objects, reducing the appeal of stargazing activities for tourists interested in astronomy. Areas with dark skies and minimal light pollution are often sought after for astronomy tourism.

Eco-tourism: Many tourists seek out destinations with pristine natural environments and wildlife habitats. Light pollution can disrupt ecosystems and wildlife behavior, detracting from the appeal of eco-tourism destinations.

Resolution Benefits

Sustainable tourism: Addressing light pollution aligns with principles of sustainable tourism by minimizing negative impacts on the environment, conserving natural resources, and promoting responsible tourism practices. Tourists increasingly seek out destinations that prioritize sustainability and environmental stewardship.

Ecotourism opportunities: Preserving dark skies and minimizing light pollution supports healthy ecosystems and wildlife habitats. Tourists visiting these areas can enjoy the natural beauty and biodiversity without disruption from excessive artificial light.
